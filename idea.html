VIS.LOG // 0x.112

The Concept.

From Abstract to Absolute

01. The Core Idea. The goal was to bridge the gap between rigid data and fluid aesthetics. This project explores how structure can be beautiful—not by hiding the "logic" of the grid, but by celebrating it.

02. Visual Language. I chose a monochromatic palette to keep the focus on form. By pairing Serif and Monospace fonts, the design speaks two languages: the emotional world of fashion and the analytical world of code.

03. Editorial Flow. Standard digital layouts often feel flat. My idea was to treat the screen like a physical magazine page, using overlapping layers and glass effects to create a tactile digital experience.

04. Calculated Tension. The asymmetric split is a deliberate move to create "visual tension." It breaks the symmetry of conventional web design to reveal a more dynamic and modern perspective.
